Editor's Note
On why we built this.

Most screenwriting software was built for desks.

Final Draft. Fade In. Highland. Celtx. They are fine tools, and they all share the same premise: you are seated, keyboard in front of you, with a block of uninterrupted hours. Scripts, the thinking goes, are written in the room.

Except they aren't. They're written at the bus stop. On the walk home. In the six minutes between meetings when a line finally lands. The phone — already in your hand — has been, for a decade, the wrong tool for the job.

Penova is the correction. Proper screenplay formatting, scene-by-scene navigation, character bibles that follow a character across every project they appear in, voice capture when typing is slower than thinking. Built around how writing actually happens, not how studios imagine it happens.

Scene Editor

Format is a grammar, not a checkbox.

Type a slug line and the editor knows the next block is action. Tab into a character cue and the next paragraph is dialogue. Industry-standard 12-point Courier, enforced margins, correct transitions — handled, invisibly, while you write.

Every element belongs to a scene. Every scene belongs to an episode or a feature. Reorder, rename, rewrite without ever leaving the phone.

The Tools — 01 / 05

What the app actually does.

01

Projects & Episodes — features, pilots, limited series

Group scripts by project. Pilot, feature, web series — each with its own cover, logline, and episode list. Drag scenes between episodes without breaking continuity.

02

Scene-first navigation — think in beats

A screenplay is a stack of scenes, not a document. Penova treats them that way: reorder, bookmark, jump. Open a scene and see its location, time of day, and every element inside it.

03

Characters across projects — a proper bible

Create a character once; reuse them across every script they appear in. Store voice, backstory, and relationships in one place — surfaced scene by scene when you need to hear them speak.

04

Quick Capture — a line before you lose it

Tap the microphone. Speak the line. Penova transcribes it on-device and files it where you tell it to go — a scene, a character's notes, or the inbox for tomorrow.

05

Export to Final Draft & PDF — industry handoff

When the draft is ready to circulate, export clean .fdx for the room or formatted PDF for the reader. Nothing proprietary. Nothing locked in.
