14INT. KITCHEN — NIGHT

Penny stands at the sink, water running. She hasn't moved in some time. A glass of red wine sweats on the counter beside her.

The front door opens. Marcus enters, his hair smelling of rain. He sets his keys down — slowly, as if a sound might break something.

MARCUS

You didn't eat.

PENNY

(without turning)

I wasn't hungry.

MARCUS

Penny.

She turns off the water. Doesn't turn around.

PENNY

I quit today.

A long beat. Marcus looks at the back of her head — at the wet ends of her hair, the small tremor in her shoulder.

MARCUS

You quit.

PENNY

I quit.
